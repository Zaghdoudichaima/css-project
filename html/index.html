Douz, Gateway to the Sahara

In the south of Tunisia, the oasis of Douz is renowned for being the gateway to the Sahara, the starting point for excursions in the desert.
Surrounded by sand dunes, the city retains its nomadic traditions and captivates with its warm and atypical atmosphere.

Tozeur

Tozeur, the largest of the oases and the most beautiful with its superb palm grove! Take the time to stroll through the narrow streets of the Medina with its brick houses with beautiful patterns and typical studded doors.
You will surely observe women there weaving beautiful carpets. Located at the gates of the desert, Tozeur will be a good starting point for a camel or 4x4 ride.

Ksar Ouled Soltane

Located in Tataouine, the ksar Ouled Soltane built in the 15th century is one of the most beautiful multi-storey attics in the country organized around two large courtyards. With its rounded shapes, its external staircases on the facade and its earth colors, this ksar was used to store the food reserves of one or more tribes. Built on a hill, the site offers beautiful views. Does this site remind you of something? You must have seen it in one of the episodes of the Star Wars saga!

Dougga

Dougga is an archaeological site, a hundred kilometers from Tunis, in excellent condition and probably one of the best preserved in the whole of the Maghreb. Classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, its remains are located on a hill and extend over more than 25 hectares. It takes half a day to observe most of the buildings such as the theater, the capitol, the thermal baths. A visit to Dougga is a step not to be missed!


Djerba

Djerba is an island off the Tunisian coast known for its Mediterranean beaches and white desert towns activated by Berber, Arab, Jewish and African cultures. Its main town is Houmt Souk. It is famous for its craft markets, its fishing port and its 16th century fortress, Borj el Kebir.
To the south is the El Ghriba Synagogue, a place of pilgrimage for North African Jews.
